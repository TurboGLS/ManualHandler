CORE2 STP 110-60 Sunspec Modbus Model PICS Overview V12

Introduction:

  • All communication is based on the Sunspec Modbus standard. Please refer to the official documentation of Sunspec Alliance: https://sunspec.org/sunspec-modbus-home/
  • A maximum of two parallel transactions with varying T-Ids is allowed.
  • Values arching over multiple registers (e.g. Model 103, Evt1, bitfield32) have to be polled completely. It is not allowed to poll only the upper or lower 16 Bits.
  • For reactive power control only one of the registers 123.OutPFSet_Ena, 123.VArPct_Ena, 126.ModEna and 131.ModEna can be activated. To change the mode, the current mode must be de-activated first.
  • Except of model 123 (Immediate Inverter Controls) all data is written to flash memory - i.e. excessive write access can damage the device.
  • As the data in model 123 is stored in RAM, it does not get restored after inverter shutdown. Cyclical writing is required.
  • Loss of PV power in the evening can lead to restart of the AC aux supply and a short termination of Modbus communication.
  • For the reading or writing of Modbus registers, use the register numbers reduced by the offset 1 in each case.
